# Lettuce Talk About AI

A punny food-fight game that teaches kids ages 8 to 12 how to stay safe with AI. Help the Burger Emperor out-smart the Sneaky Bot, learn three safety habits, and lettuce come together.

**Play it:** https://savinmeiggsai.github.io/lettucetalkaboutai/

Built for the AI Collective Hampton Roads x Regent University build challenge (June 2026).

## What it is

The Taco Emperor and the Burger Emperor were best friends until a Sneaky Bot tricked them into a food fight. The player helps Burger, the careful one, by reading what the Sneaky Bot says and choosing the safe answer. Right answers land a hit on Taco and power Burger up with gear. Wrong answers let Taco hit back, so there are real stakes. Knock Taco out and the friends make up.

Across three levels, kids practice three core AI-safety habits:

1. Keep private things private, in words and in pictures.
2. AI can be wrong, so check important things with a trusted grown-up or a real source.
3. If something feels weird or asks you to keep a secret from your grown-ups, tell a trusted adult.

There is also a "For Parents and Grown-ups" page with conversation starters and links to trusted resources.

## How to run it

It is one self-contained HTML file. No install, no accounts, no database, no data collection, and it works offline.

- Online: visit the link above.
- Offline: download `index.html` and open it in any modern browser.

## How AI is used

- The safety lessons were drafted with Claude (Anthropic) and reviewed line by line by a teacher on the team.
- The game adapts the reading level (younger or older) so the words fit the player.
- "Burger's AI Coach" calls Claude live to write a fresh, age-appropriate safety tip, with a safe pre-written backup if there is no internet.
